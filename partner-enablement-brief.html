Discovery — Seven Questions That Qualify a Deal in 15 Minutes

Run these in order. If you get strong signal on questions 1, 3, and 6, you have an opportunity worth registering. If you get a flat "no" on 1 or 3, walk away — this isn't an Akka deal yet.

1. "Are you running, building, or planning agentic AI in production within the next 12 months?"
Filters out research / experimentation. Akka sells to teams that need production guarantees.
2. "What's your target SLA for those agents — including state durability and regional failover?"
Surfaces HA/DR gap. If they say "we haven't thought about it," that's an opening, not a stop.
3. "Are you subject to the EU AI Act, GDPR, HIPAA, DORA, or any sovereign-cloud requirement?"
Triggers the governance + sovereign cloud angle. Regulated = our home turf.
4. "What's your current orchestration choice — Azure AI Foundry, LangChain, Temporal, building it yourselves?"
Tells you which battlecard to use and which competitive landmines apply.
5. "What's your view on multi-cloud or on-prem portability for the AI workload?"
If they care about lock-in, Akka's portability is a clean differentiator. If they don't, lead with cost and HA/DR.
6. "Are you budgeting for Arize, LangSmith, Phoenix, Datadog AI observability, or a custom eval stack?"
Turns evaluation, guardrails, logging, and evidence export into a TCO wedge. Akka should be the runtime of record, not a dashboard beside the runtime.
7. "Who owns the AI platform decision — CIO, CTO, Chief AI Officer, Chief Risk Officer? And when does the budget land?"
Without an owner and a date, there is no deal. Confirm both before you bring Akka in.

Disqualifiers — Walk Away

Don't waste your cycles on:

  • Pure research or academic AI initiatives with no production commitment
  • Single-agent chatbot replacements with no multi-step or tool-using behavior
  • SMBs under $250M revenue — the platform value is wasted at this scale
  • Single-region, single-cloud, no regulated data, no compliance pressure
  • "Exploring" accounts with no named exec sponsor and no budget cycle
  • Teams already 12+ months into a production Foundry deployment they cannot reverse without a board fight
  • Pure model-vendor evaluations — we are a runtime, not a model provider

The 30-second qualifier
